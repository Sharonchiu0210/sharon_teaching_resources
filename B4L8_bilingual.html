Step 1 · Why structure

Before you read, learn the shape.

在讀文章之前，先認識它的形狀。

A problem-solution article almost always moves through four parts. If you know the shape, you know what to look for — in the textbook, in your own writing, and in anything AI writes for you.

一篇 problem-solution 的文章，通常會走過四個段落。先看懂這個形狀，你才有辦法判斷課本怎麼鋪陳、自己怎麼寫，以及 AI 寫的東西哪裡有問題。

Negative impact

Why now — before reading?
AI can write a whole article in three seconds. But if you don’t know the shape, you can’t tell whether it wrote a good one. Structure is the lens.

AI 三秒鐘可以生一篇。但你不懂結構，就看不出它寫得好不好。結構，是你的判斷工具。

Step 3 · See it in action

One short example, fully labeled.

先看一個完整的小範例，每一段都標好顏色。
Introduce the problem　介紹問題
Negative impact　負面影響
Propose solutions　提出解法
Conclusion　結論

Taiwan is suffering from a phenomenon called the “science craze.” This occurs when high school students rush to pick science tracks while humanities classes shrink year by year.

Many humanities teachers are fed up with empty classrooms. Some have even been forced to switch subjects or leave the profession entirely.

To address this issue, schools could redesign humanities courses to feel more connected to real-world problems. The government could also fund cross-disciplinary programs that mix science with the arts.

If we act now, the humanities may finally regain their place in students’ futures. But real change takes time — and a different way of measuring what counts as a “useful” subject.

注意每段都有一個明確功能。第一段「給名字」，第二段「給後果」，第三段「給做法」，第四段「給判斷」。順序不能亂。
